Caesar Cipher

Caesar Cipher is one of the Mono Alphabetic Cipher wherein each letter of the plaintext is substituted by another letter to form the ciphertext. It is also referred to as the "Shift Cipher". Being a simplest form of substitution cipher scheme, the concept of Caesar Cipher is to replace each alphabet by another alphabet which is "Shifted" by some fixed number between 0 and 25.

To implement this, both sender and receiver agree on a "Secret Shift Number" for shifting the alphabets of the Plain Text. This "Secret Shift Number" which is between 0 and 25 becomes the Key of Encryption. The name ‘Caesar Cipher’ is occasionally used to describe the Shift Cipher when the "Shift of Three" i.e. when the Secret Shift Number = 3 is used.
